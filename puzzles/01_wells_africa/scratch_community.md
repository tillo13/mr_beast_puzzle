## 3. Discussion of Puzzle Mechanism

**No direct discussion found.** However, based on our own analysis of the puzzle image and general puzzle taxonomy, this puzzle appears to be a **hybrid of two classic puzzle types:**

### A. Drop Quote (Quotefall / Fallen Phrases)
A puzzle where letters are arranged above columns and must "drop" into a grid to form a hidden quote. The grid uses black squares to separate words, similar to a crossword layout.

**Standard drop quote mechanics:**
- Letters hover above each column
- Each letter drops into one of the empty squares in that column
- Black squares separate words
- When solved, reading left-to-right reveals a quote or phrase
- Letters within a column maintain their column but can go in any row

**Key strategies:**
- Start with columns that have only 1 available letter (forced placement)
- Look for common 2-letter words (OF, TO, IN, IT, IS, AT, ON, IF)
- THE, AND, FOR are the most common 3-letter words
- Contractions narrow options: apostrophe-S (HE'S, SHE'S), N'T (CAN'T, DON'T)
- Rare letters (J, K, Q, X, Z) have limited word partners
- Look for prefixes (RE-, UN-) and suffixes (-ING, -TION, -NESS)
- Each placed letter eliminates possibilities elsewhere

**Sources:**
- [Puzzazz: How to Solve Drop Quotes](http://www.puzzazz.com/how-to/drop-quotes)
- [Drop Quotes by Puzzle Baron](https://dropquotes.puzzlebaron.com/)
- [Wikipedia: Dropquote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dropquote)
- [Waflob: How to Solve Drop Quote Puzzles](https://www.waflob.com/how-to-solve-drop-quote-puzzles/)

### B. Acrostic Puzzle (Double Crostic)
A puzzle where crossword-style clues are answered, and those answer letters are transferred into a grid to reveal a hidden quote. The first letters of the clue answers typically spell something meaningful (author, title, theme word).

**Standard acrostic mechanics:**
- A set of crossword-style clues (A, B, C, D...) are listed
- Solving each clue gives a word
- Each letter in each clue answer has a numbered position
- Those numbered letters are placed into corresponding numbered squares in the main quote grid
- When the grid is complete, it reveals a hidden quotation
- **Bonus:** The first letter of each clue answer, read top-to-bottom, spells out an author name and/or work title

**Sources:**
- [Acrostic Puzzles by Puzzle Baron](https://acrostics.puzzlebaron.com/)
- [How Acrostic Puzzles Work (HowStuffWorks)](https://entertainment.howstuffworks.com/puzzles/acrostic-puzzles.htm)
- [Scrabble Solver: The Intriguing World of Acrostic Puzzles](https://www.scrabble-solver.com/blog/the-intriguing-world-of-acrostic-puzzles/)

### C. This Puzzle Likely Combines Both Mechanics

Given the puzzle image shows:
1. A grid with some letters pre-filled and water-drop-shaped blanks
2. 13 crossword-style clues labeled with letters (A through M)
3. The "wells" / water theme (drops fall = letters drop)

**Most likely mechanism:**
- Solve the 13 crossword clues to get 13 answer words
- Letters from those answers fill into the grid (acrostic-style, via numbered positions)
- OR: The clue answers provide letter banks that "drop" into columns (drop-quote style)
- The water drops in the grid represent the blank cells that need filling
- When complete, the grid reveals a hidden phrase or quote
- The 5-letter answer word may come from: reading the first letters of clue answers (acrostic), reading specific highlighted/colored cells, or reading the water-drop positions
